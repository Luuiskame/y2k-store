## 1. Executive summary

You are in an unusually strong position: **the local Honduras market for gothic / compression / Y2K gym wear is essentially uncontested on Google.** When I searched terms like *tienda ropa y2k Honduras* and *camisetas de compresión gótica*, the results were TikTok auto-pages, Etsy/Temu "Honduras-flag" tourist tees, and one cheap Instagram reseller — **no established local store owns these terms.** That is a first-mover gap you can take.

There are three things holding your current SEO back, and one strategic risk:

1. **You are building your identity on someone else's brand name.** "Breathe Divinity" is an existing, established brand (breathedivinity.com, also sold on Amazon/eBay/Etsy). You will essentially never outrank the real brand for *breathe divinity*, and the intent behind that search is mixed (most searchers want *them*). **Strategic risk + SEO ceiling.** See §2.
2. **Every page shares the exact same `meta-keywords` and nearly the same descriptions.** Your *titles* are actually good and unique — but product descriptions are generic and duplicated. Per-page uniqueness is your biggest on-page win. See §5.
3. **Your category structure is in English** (`/shirts`, `/sweatshirts`, `/pants`) for a Spanish-searching audience. Category pages are some of the strongest ranking pages an e-commerce site has, and right now they don't match how your customers actually type. See §5.4.

The plan: keep winning your branded long-tails, **pivot your primary identity to terms you can own** (your own brand + generic local high-intent terms), and build new ranking surfaces (a reflective-gear collection tied to the new traffic law, and the influencer page you described).

## 2. The "Breathe Divinity" question (read this first)

**What I verified:** Searching *breathe divinity Honduras* returns the original brand's store (breathedivinity.com — "luxury gym brand, defining the dark side"), plus Amazon, eBay, Etsy, and resellers in Mexico/Colombia. Your store and IG did not appear in my results (your localized Google Honduras may still show your IG/site for the in-country long-tail — that's believable, because almost no one else is competing on the exact string *breathe divinity Honduras*).

**Why this matters for SEO:**

- **You can't win the head term.** The real brand has years of links, traffic, and brand signals. Targeting *breathe divinity* as your primary keyword caps your ceiling and sends you traffic that's looking for someone else.
- **Mixed intent.** A chunk of *breathe divinity* searchers want the original — they may bounce off your store, which hurts your engagement signals.
- **Legal exposure.** Using another company's brand name as your product name / primary positioning carries trademark risk. I'm not a lawyer and this isn't legal advice — but before you scale spend on this, it's worth a quick consult with a Honduran IP attorney, especially if you intend to grow.

**Recommendation — don't delete it, demote it:**

- Make **"Y2K Fit"** (your own, ownable brand) the *primary* identity everywhere.
- Keep "Breathe Divinity" only as a **descriptive design-line / "inspired-by" reference** in body copy and as a *secondary* capture term (so you still catch people searching it in Honduras), never as the page's primary title token or the brand of record in structured data.
- Long-term, consider naming your in-house line something **you own** (e.g. a sub-brand) so your design line builds *your* equity instead of someone else's.

This single shift is the difference between renting traffic and owning it.

## 4. The reflective-gear opportunity (timely — but be accurate)

**What the law actually says (verified, June 2026):** In March 2026 Congress reformed the *Ley de Tránsito* (added art. 99 numeral 28). It requires **motorcyclists, their passengers, and riders of motonetas / bicimotos / bicycles** to wear a **reflective vest or a reflective band/distinctive in orange, red, or white**, **at night (6:00 p.m.–6:00 a.m.)**. It takes effect 20 days after publication in *La Gaceta*.

**Important corrections to the framing you gave me:**
- It is **not** a blanket requirement for all pedestrians/everyone. A viral claim that pedestrians must wear reflective vests was **fact-checked as false** by *La Prensa* (EH Verifica).
- The legal item is specifically a **reflective band/distinctive in orange/red/white** on a vest or harness. A gothic hoodie with reflective print is *not automatically legally compliant* unless it carries that distinctive.

**How to use this honestly (and it's still a great opportunity):**
- ✅ **Do** target the *aesthetic + visibility* angle: "hoodie reflectivo," "suéter reflectivo," "ropa con diseño reflectivo Honduras," "ropa reflectiva para moto." Tie content to the law as *context* ("con la nueva normativa, la ropa reflectiva está en tendencia").
- ✅ **If** you produce a piece that genuinely includes a reflective band/distinctive meeting the spec, *then* you can target "chaleco reflectivo motociclista Honduras" and "ropa reflectiva que cumple la ley" — and that's a high-intent, high-volume term with almost no stylish competitor.
- ⚠️ **Don't** claim a product "cumple con la ley" / "legal para motociclistas" unless it actually meets the orange/red/white reflective-distinctive requirement. Overclaiming risks customer complaints and liability.

This line deserves its **own collection page** (see §5.3) because it targets a distinct, large, and timely intent.

## 5. Website SEO — page by page

**General notes that apply site-wide (hand these to Claude Code first):**

- **`meta-keywords` is ignored by Google.** It's not hurting you, but it's not helping. Stop relying on it. The real ranking levers are: **title tag, meta description, H1, on-page text, image alt text, URL slug, internal links, and structured data (JSON-LD).** Make each *unique per page*.
- **Canonical bug to check:** your canonicals point to `https://y2kfithn.com/...` (no `/hn`, no `www`) while pages live at `https://www.y2kfithn.com/hn/...`. If the canonical URL doesn't resolve to the actual indexable URL, Google can get confused about which page to rank. **Verify the canonical matches the real, reachable URL** (including the `/hn` locale path and your preferred www/non-www).
- **Add structured data (JSON-LD)** — likely missing. Priorities: `Product` (with `offers`, price `HNL`, `availability`, `brand`), `Organization`/`LocalBusiness` (name, logo, sameAs → your TikTok/IG/FB, areaServed: Honduras), `BreadcrumbList`, and `FAQPage` on the FAQ. This is how you earn rich results (price, ratings) in Google.
- **Image alt text is wasted right now** ("Imagen del producto 1"). Every product image alt should be a natural Spanish phrase with keywords — see §5.5.
- **Hreflang/locale:** you use a `/hn` path. Make sure there's a clear default and that internal links are consistent (some footer links and product handles vary).

**The problem:** every product currently shares the *same* generic description ("Camiseta manga larga de compresion, adaptable a distintos pesos, se puede usar varias tallas, estampados de calidad") across the body, OG, and Twitter tags. Duplicate descriptions across dozens of products is the #1 thing suppressing your product pages.

**Medusa fields to use (you mentioned Medusa — here's the mapping):**

- **`title`** → Front-load the searchable term, color second. Pattern:
  `Camiseta de Compresión Manga Larga Gótica – [Color] | Y2K Fit`
  (Keep "Breathe Divinity" out of the *title token* per §2; put it in subtitle/description as the design line.)
- **`subtitle`** → secondary keywords, e.g. `Ropa deportiva gótica · tela técnica · estampado reflectivo`
- **`description`** → **write a UNIQUE 50–100 word description per product.** Include: what it is, the compression/tech detail, the gothic/Y2K design language, fit/sizing note, and a local cue ("envío a todo Honduras"). Naturally include 2–3 keywords. *No two products should share the same description.*
- **`handle` (URL slug)** → keyword-rich and consistent. You have a mix today: `breathe-divinity-manga-larga-rojo-con-negro` (good) vs `camiseta-de-compresion` (good keyword but inconsistent naming). Standardize to: `camiseta-compresion-manga-larga-[color]`. **301-redirect any slug you change.**
- **`metadata`** (Medusa custom metadata) → store per-product `meta_title` / `meta_description` and render them in the Next.js `generateMetadata()` so each product page emits a **unique** title + description instead of the shared one.
- **Image alt text** → Medusa lets you store alt text / you can derive it. Pattern: `Camiseta de compresión manga larga gótica color [color] – Y2K Fit Honduras`. Replace the current "Imagen del producto 1".
- **`Product` JSON-LD** → emit name, image, unique description, `brand: "Y2K Fit"`, `offers` with `priceCurrency: "HNL"`, `price`, `availability`. This can win you rich results (price shown in Google).
